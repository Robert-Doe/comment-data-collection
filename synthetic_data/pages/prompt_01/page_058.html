Kimchi day 3 - smells strongly of vomit/butyric acid - is something wrong?
Kimchi • Posted by KimchiNewbie_Sandra • August 2002

First time making kimchi. Used napa cabbage, Korean red pepper flakes, fish sauce, garlic, ginger, green onion, and a little sugar. Salted the cabbage overnight, rinsed, and packed into a mason jar. Day 1-2 were fine - nice fermentation bubbles. Day 3: opened the jar and was hit with a really strong, unpleasant smell - kind of like vomit or cheese. It's not the normal kimchi sour smell I expected. The appearance looks fine, no mold. I'm concerned about safety. Should I throw it out?

Sandra, the "vomit" smell is almost certainly butyric acid produced by Clostridium or Leuconostoc species during fermentation. This is a normal - if unpleasant - early-stage fermentation smell that sometimes appears in the first 2-4 days before the lactic acid bacteria become dominant. It is NOT a safety concern in kimchi where the vegetable matrix is acidifying correctly.

What to check:
1) Is the cabbage still submerged under its own liquid? Air exposure promotes unwanted bacteria.
2) What temperature is it fermenting at? Very warm conditions (above 75°F/24°C) can accelerate fermentation too fast, favoring butyric-acid producers early.

The smell should change to the characteristic sour/tangy kimchi scent as LAB take over. Give it 2 more days before deciding.

Ji-Yeon is right - butyric fermentation before LAB dominance is common, especially in warmer conditions. The smell that you're interpreting as "vomit" is recognizable to experienced fermenters as a specific transitional stage. There's a Korean saying that roughly translates to "ugly fermentation before beautiful kimchi."

One practical tip: refrigerate it now and let it continue fermenting cold (34-40°F / 1-4°C). Cold fermentation dramatically slows butyric bacteria and favors LAB. Many traditionalists do all kimchi fermentation cold (it takes longer - 2-4 weeks vs. 3-5 days - but the flavor is more complex and the smell more pleasant throughout).

Yes - add 2% salt brine (20g salt per 1 liter of non-chlorinated water) if needed to cover. Then use a weight - a small clean ziplock bag filled with the same brine works perfectly as a submerging weight. The goal is all vegetable material under the liquid surface.

At 78°F you were in overdrive fermentation territory. Refrigerator will slow everything down and the smell should resolve to normal kimchi tang in about a week. The warm-start cold-finish method is used by many Korean home fermenters intentionally.

Sandra - on the safety question: lacto-fermented vegetables like kimchi have an excellent safety record. As the pH drops below 4.6, pathogenic bacteria cannot survive. The butyric smell is unpleasant but is not an indication of harmful contamination - you're smelling a particular microbial metabolite, not spoilage. Trust the process as long as there is no actual mold visible (fuzzy growth) and the brine remains clear/pinkish, not gray or black.
