Just dropped $220 on a VG++ original pressing of Coltrane's "Blue Train" (BLP-1577). The Van Gelder stamp is crisp, the deep groove is pronounced. First play through the Rega Planar 3 was extraordinary - the bass is warm and full in a way CD simply cannot replicate. That title track with Lee Morgan on trumpet is one of the greatest recorded moments in jazz history.

The Abbey Road transfer of Art Blakey "Moanin'" (BLP-4003) is genuinely contested among collectors. I've A/B'd a deep groove mono against a 1980s Liberty reissue and the original is warmer but not by as much as the mythology suggests. The cartridge matters more than the pressing at a certain point. I'm running a Shure V15 Type III.

For anyone on a budget: Mosaic box sets are extraordinarily well mastered from the original tapes. Not cheap but considerably more affordable than originals and for serious listening rather than collecting I'd argue they're the better choice. The Blue Note 4 (or 6) CD mosaic sets are remarkable.
