Oliver, don't be embarrassed - everyone has sent wrong exchanges at contest speeds. Here's the reality:

Do the contacts count? Almost certainly yes. In CQ WW CW, the exchange is CALL + ZONE. If you sent the wrong zone, corrected yourself, and the other station logged your correct zone, the QSO is valid in your log and theirs.

Are corrections logged? No standard logging software captures the mid-QSO correction conversation. What matters is what both parties log at the end of the QSO.

How it affects your score: If you logged zone 4 and they logged zone 4, both logs agree and the QSO is a "clean" match. No penalty. The log checking software (during Cabrillo submission review) only sees your final logged data, not your in-air fumbling.

N1MM zone issue: in the station setup dialog (File > Station Data), make sure "CQ Zone" is set to 4. If the contest module doesn't auto-fill from your station profile, you can macro it into your F-key sequence. For CQ WW CW your F1 run message should be:

CQ TEST KD5XYZ KD5XYZ <CR>
Exchange: 5NN 4 <- hardcode the zone here

The <ZONE> macro pulls from station data; if it shows 0 your station data is misconfigured.
