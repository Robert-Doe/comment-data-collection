Landlord won't return security deposit after 90 days - small claims?
Posted by RentedInRaleigh • October 2002

I moved out of my apartment August 1st, left it immaculate, and my landlord has not returned my $900 security deposit 90 days later. He hasn't responded to two certified letters. My state (North Carolina) requires return within 30 days with an itemized statement. Can I sue in small claims? What do I need?

Absolutely yes, you can and should sue in small claims. North Carolina G.S. 42-52 requires the landlord to return the deposit or provide an itemized statement within 30 days. After 90 days with no response you have a very strong case. You may be entitled to the deposit PLUS damages. File in the small claims division of the District Court in the county where the rental was located. Filing fee is typically $30-50.

Keep ALL documentation: photos of the apartment when you moved out, receipts for any professional cleaning, copies of both certified letters with return receipts showing he received them, your original lease showing the deposit amount, and any move-in/move-out checklists. Judges love paper trails. Walk in with an organized binder.

Paralegal_Pat
Posts: 2,341 		Oct 16, 2002
NC GS 42-53 allows recovery of the deposit amount PLUS attorney fees if you prevail. There is no automatic treble damages provision in NC (unlike some states) but you may also include any actual damages from the non-return (e.g., expenses you incurred because you didn't have the deposit to use as down payment on a new place). Small claims limit in NC is $4,000.
