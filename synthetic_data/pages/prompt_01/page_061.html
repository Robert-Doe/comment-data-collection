Robin, the Medeco biaxial is specifically engineered to defeat SPP (single pin picking) precisely because of the dual challenge you're experiencing. Let me break it down:

Challenge 1 (standard): The pin must reach the correct height at the shear line. Same as any pin tumbler.

Challenge 2 (biaxial): Each pin must ALSO rotate to align its sidebar lug with the sidebar slot in the cylinder. Until BOTH conditions are met for ALL pins simultaneously, the sidebar prevents rotation.

What you're feeling is probably pins resetting because you haven't addressed the rotation component. The sidebar puts lateral pressure against the pins even when they're at the correct height. You need to apply rotation torque on the pins with your pick to align the lugs. This is why the Medeco is rated so high on the Locksport belt ranking.

To add practical technique: with a Medeco biaxial, you need to combine lifting and rotating pressure on each pin simultaneously. Many pickers use a dedicated Medeco-specific pick (Peterson makes one) with a slightly angled tip that applies both upward and rotational pressure in one motion. The Peterson Hook 4 isn't optimized for this.

Tension is also critical - Medeco responds very poorly to heavy tension. You want the lightest tension that still gives feedback. Many people go too heavy and the sidebar binds completely.

The rotation direction per pin corresponds to the key cut angle. On a legitimate key, the cuts are angled left, center, or right - which rotates each pin to the correct alignment. Without the key, you're trying each combination of rotations. There are three positions and typically 5-6 pins, so the search space is large.

In practice experienced pickers develop feedback feel for when the lug is aligned with the sidebar slot - there's a slight release of resistance. It takes hours of practice on a cutaway or a Medeco pinning kit to develop that feel.
