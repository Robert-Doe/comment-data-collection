Short row heel turn is leaving big holes - what am I doing wrong?
Socks • Posted by YarnStorm_Penny • December 2001

I'm knitting my first pair of socks (Magic Loop, 64 stitches, Regia 4-ply, 2.25mm needles). I've done the garter tab cast on and the leg just fine, but I'm now on the short row heel and getting big holes where I pick up the wraps. I've watched two different videos and I think I'm doing everything right but there are definitely holes and it doesn't look like the smooth heel I see in photos. Is there a trick to this?

Holes in a short row heel almost always mean you're picking up the wrap on the wrong side of the stitch, or not picking it up at all. The key: when you come to a wrapped stitch, you need to pick up BOTH the wrap AND the original stitch together and knit (or purl) them as one. On the knit side, insert the right needle through the wrap first (from below), then through the stitch, and knit together. It feels awkward at first but becomes automatic.

Also check which direction you wrapped. With most short row methods you wrap counterclockwise on a knit row and clockwise on a purl row (or the reverse, depending on the method). Cat Bordhi's and Lucy Neatby's methods handle this differently. Which video tutorial are you following?

Yes! That's exactly it. If you knit the stitch and leave the wrap behind, you get a hole because the wrap is a live loop that doesn't close. You MUST consume the wrap with the stitch every time. Silver's method is great - her written instructions are very clear on this. Re-read the pick-up section specifically. The pictures help enormously.

For beginners I actually recommend trying a flap-and-gusset heel instead of short rows for your first few pairs. The short row heel is elegant but unforgiving. The flap heel is much more intuitive, virtually eliminates the hole problem, and gives better heel durability in the final sock. Once you're comfortable you can revisit short rows with the technique knowledge you'll have gained.

If you're still getting holes after fixing the pickup technique, try Japanese short rows instead - they use a stitch marker clip instead of a yarn wrap and are much easier to work on the return. No wrap to hunt for = no hole. Interchangeable for any pattern that calls for wrap-and-turn.
