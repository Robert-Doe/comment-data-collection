Pay off debt first or invest? The eternal question
Discussion started by TightWad_Tony • Started: January 2003

I have $12,000 in credit card debt at 18.9% APR and $3,200 in a savings account earning 2.1%. I just got a raise and have $400 extra per month. Everyone in my family says invest it but the math seems obvious to me. What would you do?

Your math is exactly right. No investment reliably returns 18.9%. Paying off that card IS the investment, it's just a guaranteed 18.9% return with no risk. Once the card is gone you'll have $400 plus the minimum payment you were making freed up to invest properly. Dave Ramsey calls this "debt snowball" though he'd probably tell you to cut up the card too.

Broadly agree with Fred but with one caveat: if your employer offers a 401k match, grab that FIRST before paying extra on the debt. A 100% instant match beats even 18.9% credit card interest. So the priority order is: (1) 401k to get the match, (2) high-interest debt, (3) everything else.
