Ring ouzel - first of spring, Stanage Edge, April 4th
Peak District • Sightings Log • Posted by MoorlandMark • April 2002

Excellent find this morning on the top of Stanage Edge (SK238 836) at 08:15. A single male ring ouzel perched briefly on a gritstone outcrop before dropping into the heather. Distinctive gorget, pale wing panel visible in flight. Wind NNW F3, visibility excellent. No scope - 10x42 bins only. This is my first ring ouzel and fourth year visiting the Peak in spring hoping for one. Has anyone else recorded at Stanage this week?

Good find Mark - Stanage is one of the most reliable peak sites for ring ouzel during spring passage, typically late March to mid-April. Please submit to the Derbyshire Bird Recording Group's online form (link in sidebar). A single day at Stanage this time of year can yield 3-4 individuals if conditions are right (southerly airflow bringing Scandinavian migrants up).

I was on Higgar Tor (adjacent to Stanage) this morning and had two ring ouzels going northwest at 09:00. Almost certainly passage birds rather than local breeders this early. Did you note the extent of the pale wing panel? In female/immature birds this can be narrower and occasionally causes confusion with mistle thrush at distance.
