10" f/6 Pyrex - turned-down zone at 70% radius confirmed by Foucault - correction strokes?
Figuring • Posted by GrindingNovice_Hal • August 2001

Grinding a 10-inch f/6 Pyrex mirror from scratch. Polishing complete and I've been figuring with cerium oxide on pitch. Foucault test shows what I believe is a turned-down edge zone at approximately 70% radius - the zone appears darker than the rest at knife edge inside focus. I've been doing W strokes and 1/3 push strokes. How do I selectively correct a mid-zone turned-down area without disturbing the center or edge?

Hal, a turned-down zone at 70% radius is one of the most common figuring challenges and also one of the most correctable. Here's the diagnosis and fix:

What's happening: The 70% zone is lower than surrounding areas, causing the Foucault shadow to appear there at a knife position different from center and edge.

Correction stroke: A "W" or "figure-8" stroke centered on the 70% zone will preferentially polish the high areas (center and edge) and allow the zone to catch up. Specifically: use short strokes (1/5 to 1/6 of mirror diameter) centered exactly over the zone you want to raise. If the zone IS turned down (low), you want to remove material from outside it (surrounding high areas), not add material to the zone.

Before proceeding: confirm your Foucault reading by also doing a Ronchi test at the same knife position. Ronchi lines should bow toward the center if the zone is truly low (turned down). New ATM mirror makers sometimes misread the Foucault shadow direction. If Ronchi confirms the turned-down zone at 70%, Ernst's stroke prescription is correct.

OpticalWizard_Ernst

Exactly right. In optical figuring you can never ADD glass - you can only remove it. So correcting a low zone means lowering the surrounding high areas until they match. The stroke strategy: use a "sub-diameter" stroke that contacts primarily the areas just inside and outside the 70% zone. Work in sessions of 20-30 strokes, test after each session, and watch for the Foucault/Ronchi pattern to change.

A critical warning: DO NOT overwork. Zone correction is subtle. 20 strokes can make a significant difference. Test frequently or you'll introduce a new zone.

A practical tip for zone work: mark the pitch lap with concentric circles (a compass and a marker) at the radii corresponding to your zones. This helps you visually track where your stroke is making contact during figuring. For a 70% zone on a 10-inch mirror, mark a circle at 3.5 inches from center on your lap. Sub-diameter strokes crossing that line are what you want.
