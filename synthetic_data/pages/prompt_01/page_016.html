Searching for Kowalski family from Poznan area, 1880s
Posted by TreeHunter_Ruth • April 2001

My great-grandmother Maria Kowalska emigrated to Pennsylvania in 1893. Family records suggest she was from a village near Poznan. Her parents were listed as Stanislaw Kowalski and Jadwiga Nowak. I have searched the LDS microfilms and the online Poznan Project but have hit a wall. Has anyone researched this family?

The Poznan Project (poznan-project.psnc.pl) has indexed marriage records for the Poznan archdiocese. If Stanislaw and Jadwiga married before 1875 it may be there. Also try the Geneteka database which covers many more parishes. Kowalski is extremely common so you will need additional details like parish name or approximate birth years to narrow the search.

Your great-grandmother would have come through either Hamburg or Bremen. The Hamburg emigration lists (1850-1934) are fully indexed and searchable online. Search for Maria Kowalska departing 1892-1894. Ship manifests often list the town of origin in the German partition records. This may give you the exact village name you need.

Wonderful news! The Roman Catholic parish records for Szamotuły (parish of St. Stanislaus) survive and are held at the State Archive in Poznan. Births, marriages, and deaths from the 1820s onwards. They can be accessed by visiting the archive or ordering microfilms through the LDS Family History Library. I can assist with Polish language transcription if you find records.

Don't forget the Pennsylvania naturalization records and the 1900 and 1910 US census which will show Maria's household and may list siblings who emigrated with her or nearby. Often siblings emigrated in clusters to the same Pennsylvania mining towns. Searching for other Kowalski heads of household in the same Pennsylvania county could uncover cousins.
