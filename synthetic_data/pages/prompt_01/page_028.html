Fixed gear gearing ratio for flat city commute - 46/16 or 48/17?
Forum: Fixed Gear • Posted by FlatCityFrank • March 2003

Setting up a Bianchi Pista as my daily commuter - 8 miles each way, relatively flat (Seattle Queen Anne hill is a killer but I can walk that section). Debating between 46/16 (gear inches: 75.8) and 48/17 (74.1). The 46/16 seems more common but I've seen arguments for the 48/17 on commuting builds. What do you daily commuters actually run?

I run 46/16 on my commuter and it's a great all-round ratio for flat-ish city riding. The 48/17 is nearly identical in gear inches - you'd be splitting hairs. More important is chainline: make sure your rear cog is centered properly and your chain is tensioned correctly. Poor chainline on a fixed bike is a bigger performance issue than 1.7 gear inches.

Running 48/16 myself (78.9 gear inches) for longer commute. It's a bit more work at the start of hills but you cruise at speed much more efficiently. Depends on your average cadence preference. If you're a spinner, go lower ratio. If you push big gears, go higher.

Just make sure you're running a proper track cog (double-threaded lockring side) not a freewheel threaded onto a track hub backwards. This is a safety issue - a back-threaded freewheel can unscrew under fixed gear braking and you'll have no stopping power. Track hubs have right-hand AND left-hand threads specifically to prevent this.

The Anonymous guest point is critical. The Bianchi Pista ships with a proper Italian track hub so you should be fine, but ALWAYS check your cog and lockring are properly installed. Late to this but wanted to add: consider marking your rear QR (or bolt position) with a paint pen so you can tell if your wheel slips forward in the dropout. Even with proper tension, track bikes see more stress than road bikes and wheel slip is more common than people think.
