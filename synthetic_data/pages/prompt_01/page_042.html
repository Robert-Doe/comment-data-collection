8-month Lab absolutely will not stop pulling - tried everything
Leash Manners • Posted by LabMom_Claire • March 2002

My 8-month yellow Lab Biscuit has been pulling on the leash since day one. I've tried: stopping every time she pulls (she just stands there staring at me and then pulls again), the "be a tree" method (same result), treats (she ignores food when excited outside). She's 55lbs already and wrenching my shoulder. We're in a 6-week puppy class and the trainer says "be consistent" which I AM being but it's not working. Is this a Lab thing? Help please.

Claire, what you're experiencing is completely normal for a Lab at this age - not a breed flaw. A few things to try:

The treat problem: She's over-threshold when outside. Start in your living room, then move to just outside the front door, then the sidewalk. Teach loose-leash walking in low-distraction environments first and build up. Outside is too exciting right now for food to compete.

"Be a tree" limitation: This works when the reward is FORWARD MOTION. If she pulls and you stop, then she sits and you start again, she may be learning that sitting briefly = forward motion. The fix is to teach her that a LOOSE LEASH is what predicts forward motion. When you stop, lure her back to your side (even with body pressure), THEN start walking.

Yes - living room first! Walk a few steps, treat at your side, walk a few more, treat. The goal is she's LOOKING AT YOU anticipating the treat rather than pulling ahead. When that becomes automatic inside, move to just outside your door. This is the real loose-leash foundation. The EasyWalk: the front clip is on the chest, not the back. Leash clips to the chest ring. Use it on regular walks while you're building the trained behavior separately in low-distraction.

One more thing - exercise before training. A Lab that hasn't run is a Lab that will pull. 15-20 minutes of fetch or tug BEFORE your training walk will make a huge difference in how well she can focus. A tired Lab is a trainable Lab.

The "nothing works" feeling is completely normal at 8 months with a Lab. This is peak adolescence in dog terms. The same dog that was responding well at 4-5 months often regresses at 7-9 months as hormones and confidence kick in. This WILL pass. Stay consistent, get the EasyWalk for safety, and give it another 4-6 weeks of low-distraction foundation work. Labs generally turn a corner around 12-18 months.
