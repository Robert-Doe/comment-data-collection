1956 Royal Quiet De Luxe - platen rock hard and cracked - replacement or resurfacing?
Platens & Rollers • Posted by KeysAndCarriages_Nora • April 2001

I acquired a lovely 1956 Royal Quiet De Luxe in the original case. Everything works - escapement, shift, margin release - but the platen (the main rubber cylinder) is completely hardened with hairline cracks along the length. Type impact just bounces off it and characters are ghostly pale. I've read about "platen rejuvenators" but also about professional resurfacing and outright replacement. What are my options and which is best for a machine I want to use regularly?

Nora, the three options in order of preference for a regularly-used machine:

1. Professional resurfacing (best): A platen restorer removes the old hardened rubber and applies new rubber compound. Cost $20-40 depending on the shop. The result is like a new platen. Swisher Ribbon & Typewriter in Cleveland does excellent work by mail order. JJ Short Associates in New York is another. Turnaround 2-3 weeks.

2. Replacement: If an original Royal QDL platen can be found (eBay, parts machines), this is quick but the replacement may also be hardened. Functional if fresh.

3. Platen rejuvenators: Products like Platen Revitalizer or WD-40 can temporarily soften a platen but this is not a lasting fix and can introduce stickiness. For a machine you'll use regularly, I don't recommend it.

The Royal Quiet De Luxe is one of the most beloved portables ever made. Worth the investment of a proper resurfacing. The feed rollers (smaller rubber rollers that guide paper) may also be hardened - check those too while you have the machine out. They cause paper-feeding problems that look unrelated. JJ Short will resurface all rollers for a package price.

You can mail just the platen. Royal QDL platen removal: turn the knob on the right side of the platen shaft counterclockwise until the platen slides out of the carriage from the left. The platen knobs unscrew (left-hand thread on the right knob - righty-loosey on that side). Wrap in bubble wrap, send to the shop with a note describing the machine model and year. The shop will return the resurfaced platen which drops back in. Total time in your hands: 10 minutes.

While you have the platen out, it's a good time to clean the type slugs with a stiff brush dipped in denatured alcohol. Old ink fills the letter forms and blurs the type. A toothbrush and some patience on each key face will dramatically improve print quality even beyond the platen improvement. The QDL has beautiful typeface - worth cleaning properly.
