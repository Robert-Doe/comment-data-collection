DO NOT EAT THESE.
Based on your description - orange/red cap, free white gills, ring/skirt on stem, CUP (volva) at base, under conifers - this matches the profile of Amanita species very closely. Several Amanita species in California with this appearance are DEADLY. The cup (volva) at the base is the most critical identification feature of deadly Amanitas. This is not a situation for online ID.

Please photograph the cup clearly and take a specimen to your local mycological society for in-person identification. Under NO circumstances eat these based on anyone's online opinion.

Dana is absolutely right. At 4500ft in northern California under Ponderosa pine, you are in prime Amanita muscaria (fly agaric) and potentially Amanita phalloides (death cap) territory. I cannot stress strongly enough: the volva/cup is the hallmark of deadly Amanitas. The fact that it "looks like something from a restaurant" is not relevant - edible species and deadly species can be visually similar to inexperienced eyes.

The California Mycological Society (myceliumwest.org) can help with in-person ID.

Good decision. The fourth photo confirms a prominent volva. These are almost certainly Amanita muscaria var. formosa or a related species. Amanita muscaria is poisonous (though rarely fatal in adults) but can be deadly in large quantities or to children. The death cap (A. phalloides) requires a spore print and microscopy to distinguish. Just don't eat them.
