Help me decide: Panasonic PT-AE500 vs Sony VPL-HS20 for 100" screen basement HT
Projectors • Posted by BasementCinema_Greg • November 2003

Setting up a dedicated basement home theater: 100" 1.0 gain screen (Stewart Studiotek 130 tentatively), 9-foot viewing distance, room is light controlled. Budget $2,500 for projector. The PT-AE500 is LCD at 1500 lumens, the VPL-HS20 is LCOS/D-ILA at 1000 lumens but supposedly much better black levels. Room is sealed dark but I can't decide if lumens or black level matters more for a 9-foot throw.

For a sealed dark room at 9-foot throw on a 100" 1.0 gain screen: black levels win every time. Your eyes at 9 feet in a dark room are exquisitely sensitive to lifted blacks. The PT-AE500 is a good projector but its native contrast (~600:1) will show grey blacks in dark scenes. The HS20's LCOS technology gives ~1800:1 native contrast and the blacks in a dark room are genuinely black. 1000 lumens is more than enough for a sealed basement - you'll actually need to neutral-density filter it at 9 feet on that screen.
LCDvsLCOS

The other factor to consider: LCD projectors (PT-AE500) can develop a "screen door effect" where the pixel structure is visible at close viewing distances. At 9 feet on 100" you're at ~1.1x screen height viewing distance which is relatively close. LCOS has a much finer fill factor (no black pixel gaps) so the image appears more "film-like" at close range.

Studiotek 130 (1.3 gain) + HS20 (1000 lumens) at 9ft on 100": you'll measure approximately 14-16 foot-lamberts, which is right in the SMPTE sweet spot (12-16 fL). That's actually ideal. Don't chase a high-gain screen - it narrows your viewing cone and hot-spots. The ST130 is the right call. Stewart screens are unmatched for uniformity at any price.
