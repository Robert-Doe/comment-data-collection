Juno-106 recap complete but now ALL 6 voices silent - did I damage the 80017A chips?
Roland Repair • Posted by SynthCollector_Finn • July 2002

Just completed a full recap on my Roland Juno-106. Original caps replaced with Nichicon and Panasonic equivalents, same values. But now ALL six voices are completely silent - no audio whatsoever, though the synth powers on, the display works, and the control panel responds normally. Before the recap, I had 2 failed voices (typical 80017A chip failure). The other 4 were working. Now none work. I'm worried I've damaged the 80017A chips with static or heat. What should I check first?

Finn, all 6 voices going silent after a recap points to a common-path problem, not 6 individual chip failures. The 80017A chips are unlikely to have been damaged by the recap itself unless you used a heat gun near them (don't). More likely:

1) POWER RAIL ISSUE: Check the +5V, +15V, and -15V rails with a multimeter before doing anything else. A shorted or wrong-value capacitor on a power supply rail can collapse the rail and silence all voices simultaneously. Measure at the PSU test points shown on the service manual page 8.

2) GROUND CONNECTION: If a ground point was disturbed during recap, all voices can drop out.

3) REINSTALLED CAP BACKWARDS: A reversed electrolytic cap on the voice board supply rail will kill all voices and may blow the cap. Look for any cap that appears swollen/leaking.

Victor is right - start with power rails. On the Juno-106 specifically, also check the CPU/DCB section. The 80017A chips require the voice assign CPU to tell them which voice to activate. If the CPU lost its reference voltage (from a recap error in that section) it won't send voice gate signals and all voices will be silent even if the 80017A chips are intact. This is different from the typical "one quiet voice" 80017A failure mode.

VCO_Victor
Posts: 8,112 		Jul 12, 2002
THAT IS YOUR PROBLEM. C47 reversed is a textbook all-voices-out failure on the 106 voice board. The stripe on an electrolytic cap marks the NEGATIVE lead. If the service manual shows stripe-up in that position, the negative lead goes up = the board layout has ground at the top of that cap's footprint. BEFORE you power on again: desolder C47 immediately, replace with a fresh cap installed correctly. A reversed electrolytic run under power will fail (sometimes dramatically) and can take out other components. Do not power on with it reversed.

Important habit for all future recap work: after installing EVERY electrolytic cap, before powering on, visually compare your placement to the service manual silkscreen photo or the cap polarity marks on the PCB. Some boards mark the + side, some mark the - side (stripe). Know which convention your board uses before starting. A 5-minute check per session prevents exactly this problem.

The Juno-106 service manual is available at Synthfool.com as a free PDF. It has excellent cap polarity markings. For future reference: the 80017A chips in the Juno are actually the Roland-proprietary BA662A VCA + IR3R05 DCO combination in a single package. They fail due to the epoxy coating cracking and allowing moisture in. They are being reproduced by Analogue Renaissance if you still need to replace your 2 failed voices after fixing the recap.
