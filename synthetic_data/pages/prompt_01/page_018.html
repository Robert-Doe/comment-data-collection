Hypothyroidism and extreme fatigue - am I doing something wrong?
By TiredInOhio • February 2002

I was diagnosed with hypothyroidism 6 months ago and have been on 75mcg Synthroid since October. My TSH is now within normal range (2.1) but I am still exhausted every single day. I sleep 10 hours and still can't function before noon. My doctor says the labs are fine so there's nothing wrong. Am I missing something? Has anyone else experienced this?

ThyroidWarrior_Sue — Posts: 1,342 — Feb 14, 2002
The TSH range is extremely controversial in the hypothyroid community. Many endocrinologists now believe optimal TSH for thyroid patients is between 0.5 and 1.0, not the lab "normal" of 0.5-4.5. At 2.1 you may still be undermedicated for YOUR body. Push for a dose increase to 88 or 100mcg and retest in 6-8 weeks. Also ask for Free T3 and Free T4 to be tested, not just TSH.

A GP can order them but some resist. Simply say "I'd like to have a full thyroid panel including Free T3 and Free T4 at my next blood draw." If they refuse, ask why. You have the right to request tests. Many of us find we need to be our own advocates. If they won't budge, seek a second opinion.

Have you been tested for Hashimoto's thyroiditis? Many hypothyroid cases are autoimmune in origin and testing for thyroid antibodies (TPO and Tg antibodies) is important. If you have Hashimoto's there are dietary approaches (gluten-free, selenium supplementation) that some find helpful alongside medication.

EndocrinologyMD — Posts: 2,108 — Feb 16, 2002
[Moderator Note: This is not medical advice] As a physician who participates in this forum I want to add some balance: TSH of 2.1 is genuinely within a healthy range for most patients. That said, the optimal level does vary individually. Fatigue with normal TSH also warrants investigation for other causes: iron deficiency (ferritin, not just hemoglobin), vitamin B12, vitamin D, adrenal function. These are often undertested in hypothyroid patients.

Thank you for that balanced perspective. The ferritin point is especially important - hypothyroid patients frequently have low ferritin even with normal hemoglobin and this alone causes profound fatigue. TiredInOhio, ask specifically for ferritin (NOT just iron or CBC).
