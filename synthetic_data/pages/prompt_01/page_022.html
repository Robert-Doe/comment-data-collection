CO2 diffuser placement in 55gal planted - getting poor distribution
Forum: Planted Tanks • Posted by FernFarmer • July 2003

Running a pressurized CO2 setup on my 55 gallon. Milwaukee MA957 regulator, 5lb tank, Rhinox diffuser placed on the left rear wall at mid-height. My drop checker is showing dark green/yellow all day which I think means CO2 is high? But my plants aren't pearling and I have some algae starting on the glass. Should I move the diffuser?

Dark green/yellow on the drop checker means you're already at high CO2 - the target is bright green (approximately 30ppm). Yellow means you're OVER-dosing which will stress fish and can kill them. Back off the bubble rate immediately. As for placement: the diffuser should go near a powerhead or filter outlet so the microbubbles get distributed across the whole tank before they reach the surface. Left rear with filter outlet on right = poor distribution.

+1 to Dieter. Also your algae is a classic sign of CO2 fluctuation, not necessarily levels. CO2 should be on a timer - same hours as your lights. Fish need O2 at night when plants aren't photosynthesizing. A consistent CO2 level that plants can use efficiently will outcompete algae.

Yes, place the diffuser 2-3 inches in front of and slightly below the filter return nozzle. The flow will carry the microbubbles across the tank. Rhinox diffusers produce excellent bubble size if you're getting good pressure. What bubble rate are you running? Count bubbles per second.

For a 55gal I'd aim for 2-3 bubbles/second starting point. Also are you running any surface agitation? Surface agitation degasses CO2 fast. If you have a powerhead or wavemaker pointing at the surface you're fighting yourself.
