Accidentally processed green beans for only 15 minutes in pressure canner - safe?
Food Safety • Posted by NewCanner_Beth • August 2001

I processed 7 quarts of green beans today and made a mistake. My recipe says 25 minutes at 10 lbs pressure for quarts, but I miscounted and only processed for 15 minutes. I realized when I was writing in my canning journal. The jars all sealed (I can hear the "ping" and the lids are concave). Should I throw them out? Can I reprocess? I know botulism is no joke with low-acid vegetables. Located in Denver (altitude about 5,400 ft).
  Canning Safety Responses (8)
MasterCanner_Joyce — Master Food Preserver — Posts: 6,882 — Aug 14, 2001

Beth, do NOT eat those jars as they are. Please reprocess or discard them.

Green beans are a low-acid vegetable - the single highest-risk category for Clostridium botulinum in home canning. The 25-minute processing time at 10 lbs is based on USDA heat penetration testing to destroy botulism spores in the center of a quart jar. 15 minutes is insufficient.

A sealed lid does NOT mean safe - sealed jars can still contain botulism toxin. The seal only means the jar is vacuum-sealed, not that it's sterilized.

Your options:
1) Reprocess within 24 hours: refrigerate jars immediately, open, bring beans back to boil, repack in fresh hot jars with new lids, and process for the full 25 minutes (plus altitude adjustment - see below).
2) Discard: the beans are not worth a botulism risk.
AltitudeCanner_Sue — Posts: 2,101 — Aug 14, 2001

Joyce is right, and I want to add your altitude information: at 5,400 ft in Denver you should be processing green beans at 11 lbs pressure (not 10 lbs) for 25 minutes. Water boils at a lower temperature at altitude, so the pressure must be increased to achieve the same internal temperature. At 10 lbs/5,400 ft you may not have even reached adequate sterilization temperature. The USDA altitude chart shows 10-12 lbs for 2,001-4,000 ft, 12-14 lbs for 4,001-6,000 ft (weighted gauge), or 15 lbs for 1,001+ ft (dial gauge depending on type).

Thank you for responding so quickly. I did not know about the altitude pressure adjustment - I've been using 10 lbs all summer! How do I know what type of gauge I have? My canner is an All American 921 (21 quart). And can you confirm: reprocessing within 24 hours makes the jars safe?
MasterCanner_Joyce — Posts: 6,882 — Aug 14, 2001

All American canners use a weighted gauge (the weight that jiggles). For weighted gauge canners, USDA recommends 15 lbs pressure at all altitudes above 1,000 ft. The weighted gauges only come in 5, 10, and 15 lb weights - so for Denver you use the 15 lb weight, period.

Yes: reprocessing within 24 hours is fully safe per USDA - use fresh new lids and full processing time. The quality will be slightly reduced (softer texture) but safety will be correct. Please also reprocess or discard any other low-acid vegetables (corn, beets, carrots, potatoes) you may have processed at 10 lbs this summer.

Many older family recipes and even some newer books use times that have not been validated. When in doubt, USDA. This forum links the PDF in our resources section.
