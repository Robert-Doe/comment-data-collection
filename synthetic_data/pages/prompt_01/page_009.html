Oh dear I think my tomatoes have got blight. The leaves started going dark brown at the edges about a week ago and now the whole lower half of the plant looks awful. I've been growing Moneymaker variety for three years without problems. We've had an unusually wet July. Is there anything I can do to save the fruit that's already set? Some of the tomatoes are about golf ball size.

I'm afraid once blight takes hold there's not much you can do for the affected foliage, Marge. Your best bet is to remove all affected leaves immediately and destroy them - do NOT compost them. For the fruit that's already set, pick anything that has any colour showing and ripen indoors. Spray the remaining healthy growth with a copper-based fungicide such as Bordeaux Mixture which you can get at most garden centres. Prevention really is better than cure with blight, unfortunately. Wet summers like this one always cause problems.

I had the same problem last year. What Ted says is exactly right. I'd also suggest next year trying a blight-resistant variety like Ferline or Legend. I switched to Ferline this year and touch wood, no problems despite this dreadful weather. You'll never get the same flavour as Moneymaker but at least you'll have a harvest!
