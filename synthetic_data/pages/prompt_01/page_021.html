Help! Vintage Simplicity 4082 sizing doesn't match my measurements at all
Posted by NeedleAndThimble_Bev • September 2002

I found a Simplicity pattern from 1967 at an estate sale (pattern #4082, A-line shift dress) and the size 16 on the envelope says bust 36" but when I traced and cut a size 16 the pieces are enormous. Modern size 16 is bust 38". Has vintage pattern sizing always been so different? Should I be grading down?

Vintage pattern sizing is VERY different from modern sizing. In the 1960s a "size 16" had a 36" bust. Today's "size 16" is approximately 38". This is called "vanity sizing" and it's been creeping upward since the 1970s. With a vintage pattern you need to measure the actual tissue pieces and compare to your body measurements, not trust the envelope size at all. Add 2-3" ease for the era's style.

Pat is exactly right. I have a reference chart somewhere I'll try to find. As a rule of thumb: 1960s Simplicity/McCall's size chart runs about 2 sizes smaller than modern RTW. So if you wear a modern size 12, look for vintage patterns in size 14. Also 1960s patterns have VERY little wearing ease - they were meant to be fitted close to the body, which was the fashion.

Bev - measure the tissue paper pieces at the bustline, not at the seam allowance (usually 5/8"). The finished garment measurement should be your bust + 2" minimum ease for a fitted shift. At 37" bust, you want the tissue to measure about 39" across the bust pieces combined.
