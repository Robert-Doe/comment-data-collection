Darkroom Chemistry: Getting the Most from Rodinal
Posted in Film Photography • February 2003

Rodinal has been around since 1891 and it is still one of the sharpest, most reliable developers you can use. Share your dilution ratios, stand development times, and favourite film pairings below.

  Community Responses
FP

FilmPurist 		For Kodak Tri-X at 400 ISO I use 1+50 dilution at 20C for 11 minutes with gentle inversions every minute. Grain is beautiful and the tonal range is exceptional. For 1+100 stand development I go 60 minutes without any agitation after the first minute. Completely different look but equally valid.
Feb 14, 2003
ZW

Zone_Worker 		Rodinal 1+25 with HP5 Plus at box speed gives a cracking negative. Sharp as a razor with gorgeous highlights. The downside is grain is quite prominent so it's not for everything. I use it for landscapes where I want that gritty, textured look. For portraits I switch to something finer-grained.
Feb 15, 2003
CB

ChambersBob 		I've been experimenting with semi-stand development for 4x5 sheet film. Rodinal 1+150 for 90 minutes, one gentle inversion at 45 minutes. The compensation effect is remarkable for high-contrast outdoor scenes. Shadows open up beautifully without blowing the highlights. Adams would have loved this.
