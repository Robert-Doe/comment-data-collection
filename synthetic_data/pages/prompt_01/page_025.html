DCC decoder install in Atlas GP38-2 - no room for any decoder I try?
HO Scale DCC • Posted by SwitchYard_Steve • April 2002

I have an older Atlas GP38-2 (the DC/analog version from the mid-90s run) and I'm trying to install a Digitrax DH163 decoder. The chassis block is solid metal and there's no DCC socket, obviously. I've removed the DC motor brushes and wired the decoder but it barely fits under the shell and the shell won't seat properly. Has anyone done this install? Any tricks?

Steve, the older Atlas GP38-2 has a very tight shell clearance over the weight block. The trick that works for most people: mill or file the top of the weight block down by about 1/16" in the area where the decoder sits (usually above the motor). This gives you the room you need. You can also try a smaller decoder - the Digitrax DH123 is thinner and works well in the GP38-2. Keep your DH163 for a bigger locomotive.
AtlasOwner_Jim

Frank is right about the block milling. I used a Dremel with a carbide burr. Takes maybe 10 minutes and the results are very clean. If you don't have a Dremel, a flat file and patience works too. Also make sure your motor leads are routed flat along the chassis - even a little loop of wire will prevent the shell from seating.

DCCWizard_Frank
Posts: 3,204
Layout Master 		Apr 4, 2002
ABSOLUTELY isolate the decoder from the block. Wrap the decoder in heat-shrink tubing or Kapton tape before placing it on the block. Kapton tape is better because it's very thin and heat-resistant. You can get it from electronics suppliers. Without isolation you WILL destroy the decoder the moment you put the loco on the track.

Also worth mentioning: pre-test the decoder on the bench with a test track before installing in the loco. Power up, read back CV8 (manufacturer ID), and set the loco address. That way if something is wrong with the decoder you know before you've wrestled it into the chassis.
