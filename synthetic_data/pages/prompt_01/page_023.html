Posted by KD9XRM_Tony • June 2002

I'm new to SSTV (KD9XRM, Tech/General class, Yaesu FT-847, Carolina Windom on 40m). Downloaded MMSSTV and it works great on receive. Wanting to start transmitting. I see many different modes listed - Martin 1, Martin 2, Scottie 1, Scottie 2, PD120, etc. Which is most commonly used on HF these days and which frequency do people gather on 20m?

Welcome to SSTV, Tony! On 20m the main SSTV calling frequency is 14.230 MHz (USB). Martin 1 is the most widely used mode on HF by a substantial margin - it's 114 seconds per frame, good quality, and your TX will be recognizable to the most operators. Scottie 1 is also common and very similar quality. Avoid PD modes on crowded 20m - they take too long. When receiving on 14.230, let MMSSTV auto-detect the mode (it does this very well).

From VK-land I can add: 14.230 is indeed the standard. On 80m it's 3.733 MHz in most regions. When you first transmit, send something simple - your callsign and locator grid. The SSTV community is very welcoming to new stations. Also join the SSTV mailing list, there are scheduled net sessions most weekends where newcomers are particularly welcome.

Tony - yes, USB + soundcard is the correct approach. The FT-847 has no built-in SSTV mode. Connect your soundcard output to the mic input on the radio (or use the DATA port if you have a SignaLink or similar interface). Drive level: start LOW. Watch your ALC meter on the rig - it should barely move. Over-driving produces horrible splatter. Your first TX will be appreciated more for being clean than loud!
