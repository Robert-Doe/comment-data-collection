# Prompt 34 — Social Commerce Hybrid Reviews (Instagram Shopping / Shopify, 2020–Present)

**Target pages:** 100  
**Output dir:** `synthetic_data/pages/prompt_34/`  
**Era:** 2020–present (Instagram Shopping, Shopify Product Reviews app, Okendo, Loox, Judge.me)  
**UGC type:** Social commerce hybrid reviews — photo UGC + text + social proof elements  
**Label:** Positive (UGC region present)

---

## LLM Generation Prompt

Generate a complete, self-contained HTML page representing a Shopify-era DTC brand product page review section. These use apps like Okendo, Loox, Judge.me, or Stamped.io to collect and display photo/video reviews alongside social proof metrics ("X people bought this today"). The aesthetic is clean, modern, and strongly influenced by Instagram — circular avatars, photo thumbnails in a row, minimal typography, and heavy use of social proof signals. Each of the 100 pages must vary across ALL of the following dimensions.

### Structural Dimensions to Vary
- Number of reviews: 8–30
- Photo UGC strip: horizontal scrollable row of customer photo thumbnails above the individual reviews (common Loox / Okendo pattern) — `<div class="photo-strip">` with `<img>` thumbnails, each overlaid with reviewer initials
- Review card contents: circular avatar, reviewer name + city, star rating, photo thumbnail(s), review title, review body, "Verified Buyer" badge, date, helpful vote count
- Attributes collected: "What I love:", "Would recommend to:", "My skin type:", "Size purchased:" — structured attribute fields below review body (Okendo-style)
- "Powered by [Platform]" footer: "Powered by Okendo", "Verified by Loox", "Reviews by Judge.me"
- Social proof banner: "⭐ 4.8 · 2,847 reviews · 94% recommend" at top of section
- "Community Photos" section header for photo strip
- Filter chips: "All Stars | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | With Photos | Verified Buyers"
- "Share your experience" CTA button: `<button class="btn-primary">Write a review</button>`
- Incentive banner on some pages: "Earn 50 points for leaving a photo review"

### HTML/CSS Characteristics (Shopify theme / app output 2020–present)
- DOCTYPE: `<!DOCTYPE html>`
- Shopify-specific attributes: `data-section-id`, `data-section-type="reviews"` on section container
- App-specific class prefixes: `okeReviews_*` (Okendo), `loox-*` (Loox), `jdgm-*` (Judge.me), `spr-*` (Shopify Product Reviews legacy)
- Shopify Liquid comment: `<!-- /snippets/product-reviews.liquid -->` or `<!-- okendo-reviews-widget -->`
- CSS: CSS custom properties from theme (`--color-accent: #C8A96E; --font-body: 'DM Sans', sans-serif;`) plus app-specific styles
- Google Fonts or Shopify CDN fonts: `<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=DM+Sans&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">`
- Photo strip: `display: flex; overflow-x: auto; gap: 8px; scroll-snap-type: x mandatory;` with `scroll-snap-align: start` on each photo
- Review cards: `display: grid; grid-template-columns: auto 1fr;` or flex-based
- ARIA: `aria-label="Customer reviews"`, `role="list"`, `role="listitem"`
- Schema.org JSON-LD: always present, generated by the review app
- Lazy loading: `loading="lazy"` on all review photos
- Dark mode on some pages (Shopify themes increasingly support it)

### Content Dimensions to Vary
- DTC brand categories: skincare/beauty, apparel, supplements, home goods, pet products, coffee/food, candles, jewelry — typical Shopify DTC categories
- Review app variant: Okendo (attributes + NPS), Loox (photo-first), Judge.me (open source feel), Stamped (loyalty points), legacy Shopify Product Reviews (minimal)
- Customer photo quality: some reviews have multiple photos (product in use), some have no photos
- Attribute fields: beauty-specific ("Skin type: Dry", "Age range: 30–40"), apparel-specific ("Height: 5'6\"", "Size purchased: M"), food-specific ("Occasion: Gift", "Frequency: Daily")
- Review language: mostly English, some with Spanish or French entries (DTC brands often sell internationally)

### Output Format
Single complete HTML file. Style it as a DTC brand product page review section — clean, aspirational, Instagram-influenced. Include the horizontal photo strip at the top, then individual review cards. Use app-specific class name patterns from one of the review platforms. Include `<script type="application/ld+json">` structured data. Show the social proof metrics prominently.
